The 7-11-4
Rule

Before anyone buys from you, they need 7 hours of engagement across 11 touchpoints in 4 separate locations. This is Google's research — and most coaches are failing at all three numbers.

captainai.com/7-11-4 · Ian Kirk · 2026

The Three Numbers
7
Hours
7 Hours of Engagement
Cumulative time a prospect spends with your content before they trust you enough to buy.
11
Touchpoints
11 Separate Encounters
Distinct moments where a prospect encounters you or your brand — each one is a separate event.
4
Locations
4 Separate Channels
The touchpoints need to span at least four different platforms or contexts to build multi-dimensional credibility.
The Insight

Why the DMs Don't Convert

What most coaches do
  • Post once, expect leads
  • DM cold with an offer immediately
  • Live on one platform only
  • No email list, no follow-up
  • Wonder why "nobody buys"
What 7-11-4 requires
  • Consistent long-form content (hours)
  • Multiple touchpoints before the ask
  • Presence across 4 channels
  • Email list to close the loop
  • A system — not a one-off post

Most coaches post and wonder why nobody buys. They've given people 6 minutes of content, not 6 hours. The math doesn't work. One post that 10,000 people see once is less valuable than 11 posts the right 100 people see regularly.

Self-Audit

Audit Your Own Numbers

  • Hours: Is it close to 7?
    Add up the total runtime of your last 30 days of content. Videos, posts, Lives. Is it close to 7 hours for an engaged follower?
  • Touchpoints: Are you at 11?
    How many separate times did your ideal buyer encounter you last month? Count every post, every reply, every DM, every email.
  • Channels: Can someone find you in 4 places?
    Could someone who's never heard of you find you in 4 different places? Or are you a one-platform person?
The Fix

If any number is low, the fix isn't to post more of the same thing. Identify which channel is missing, which format would add the most hours, and where you're losing people between touchpoints. That's the session Ian runs with clients — and it takes 90 minutes.
