## Chat vs Cowork vs Code (Quick Version)

Covered in detail in another post in this course. Here's the 30-second version.

- **Claude Chat** is your thinking partner. Conversations and quick work. Where 80% of your AI time happens.
- **Claude Cowork** is your digital coworker. Multi-step tasks on your files. Recurring scheduled work.
- **Claude Code** is your power tool. Advanced automation, custom MCPs, building systems.

Decision shortcut.

- Want a response? Chat
- Want a task done? Cowork
- Want a system built? Code

Most of you live in Chat. Add Cowork when you have repeatable work. Move to Code when you want to build.

## The Models (And When to Use Each)

Claude isn't one model. It's a family. You pick which one Claude uses.

- **Claude Opus 4.7.** The newest flagship. Most powerful reasoning. Use for complex strategy, deep analytical work, important content, multi-step thinking.
- **Claude Opus 4.6.** Previous flagship. Still excellent. Good fallback when 4.7 is at capacity.
- **Claude Sonnet 4.6.** The workhorse. Balanced power and speed. Use this for 80% of your daily work. It's fast, smart, and easier on your usage budget.
- **Claude Haiku 4.5.** Fast and cheap. Good for simple tasks, quick lookups, or building agents that need to move fast.

My rule of thumb. Sonnet 4.6 is your default. Switch to Opus 4.7 only when the work needs maximum brainpower (a strategy doc, a complex offer build, a deep client situation). Opus burns way more of your token budget per response.

You can change models in the model selector at the top of any chat.

## Don't Burn Your Tokens

A lot of people upgrade to Pro, burn through their entire usage limit in 2 days, and then complain. Don't be that person.

Token-saving habits.

- Default to Sonnet 4.6, not Opus. Switch to Opus only when the work demands it.
- Be specific in your prompts. Vague prompts get vague answers, which means more back-and-forth, which burns more tokens.
- Use Memory and CLAUDE.md so you're not re-explaining context every chat.
- Use Skills for repeatable workflows (covered in the Skills post).
- Don't dump entire 90-minute transcripts when you only need a summary of one section. Trim before you upload.
- Start a fresh chat when you switch topics. Long, sprawling chats burn more tokens per response than focused ones.

If you're on Pro and constantly hitting limits, you've got two options. Get more disciplined with the habits above, or upgrade to Max. Both work.

## Stop Pinning Chats. Do This Instead.

This is the biggest mistake people make with Claude.

They have a great strategy session. They pin the chat to the top of their sidebar. They keep coming back to it day after day, week after week, talking back into the same long-running thread.

Here's what actually happens when you do that.

The context window fills up. Claude starts losing track of what was said earlier. Responses get slower. Then the hallucinations start. You ask Claude about something you covered three weeks ago and it makes up an answer that sounds confident and is completely wrong.

The chat gets dumber the longer it lives. Pin it and you'll trust it. Trust it and you'll get burned.

Don't pin chats. Set up Projects.

A Project in Claude is a workspace with persistent context. You drop in files, set custom instructions once, and every chat inside that Project inherits all of it. The instructions and files stay. The chats are disposable.

The pattern that wins. Open a Project. Start a new chat. Do the work. Close the chat. Tomorrow, open the same Project. Start a new chat. Pick up fresh.

Projects are persistent. Chats are disposable. Lock that pattern in.

Set up 3 to 5 Projects for the workflows you run constantly.

- Weekly planning
- Content creation (Skool posts, emails, social)
- Client X strategy
- Offer build
- Daily journaling

Don't overthink the setup. Name the Project. Drop in any reference files you've got (your offer, your brand voice, your client list). Add a 3 to 5 line custom instruction. Done. You can refine it as you go.

Now when you open Claude, your most important workflows are right there in the sidebar. Open the Project. Start a new chat. Get to work.

I run 8 Projects. They're the backbone of my week.

## Projects vs Skills (Quick Clarification)

You just set up Projects. Skills are a different layer.

- **Projects** are the workspace. Persistent context, files, instructions. You set up 3 to 5 of these.
- **Skills** are reusable workflow recipes Claude follows when triggered. Great for repeatable processes (planning the week, writing a sales script, generating content).

Use them together. Run a Skill inside a Project for compounding power.

Skills get their own post in this course.

## Your Daily Winning Formula

If you don't know where to start, run this for 7 days.

**Morning (5 to 10 mins):**
- Open your daily planning Project. Start a new chat.
- Tell Claude what's on your plate today
- Have it pull your calendar, surface priorities, time-block your day

**Midday (as needed):**
- Use Claude to draft anything you'd normally write yourself (emails, posts, scripts)
- Use it to research before any sales call or strategy session
- Use it to summarize anything that takes more than 10 minutes to read

**End of day (5 mins):**
- Open your EOD Project. Start a new chat.
- Tell Claude what got done, what didn't, what you're stuck on
- Have it surface tomorrow's top 3 priorities

Run this for 7 days. By day 8, you won't be able to imagine working without Claude.
